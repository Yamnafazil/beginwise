Learning Skills

Let's Embrace the Journey of Learning!

Kalma Recitation

Instructions

  1. Teach your kids the Pehla Kalma: "La ilaha illallah, Muhammadur Rasoolullah."
  2. Explain the meaning: "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah."
  3. Repeat each part after you and practice daily, 5 times in the morning and evening.
  4. Review daily, especially during prayers or bedtime.

Colors Name

Instructions

  1. Teach your kids basic colors: Red, Blue, Yellow.
  2. Use visual aids like pictures, toys, and everyday objects.
  3. Repeat color names during daily activities (e.g., "This apple is red").
  4. Sing songs and rhymes about colors.
  5. Use coloring books and crayons for hands-on learning.
  6. Consistently name and point out colors throughout the day.
  7. Encourage your child to name colors of objects around them.
  8. Praise and reward them when they correctly identify colors.

Vegetables Names

Instructions

  1. Teach your kids basic vegetable names: Carrot, Broccoli, Potato, Tomato.
  2. Use pictures or actual vegetables as visual aids.
  3. Repeat vegetable names during meals and grocery shopping.
  4. Sing songs and rhymes about vegetables.
  5. Use coloring books and draw different vegetables.
  6. Consistently name and point out vegetables throughout the day.
  7. Encourage your child to name vegetables they see.
  8. Praise and reward them when they correctly identify vegetables.

Fruits Name

Instructions

  4. Sing songs and rhymes about fruits.
  5. Use coloring books and draw different fruits.
  6. Consistently name and point out fruits throughout the day.

Counting 1-10

Instructions

  1. Teach your kids to count from 1 to 10.
  2. Use visual aids like number cards or fingers.
  3. Repeat numbers during daily activities (e.g., counting steps or toys).
  4. Sing songs and rhymes about counting.
  5. Play interactive counting games.
  6. Use coloring books with numbers and let them trace or color them.
  7. Consistently count objects around the house.
  8. Encourage your child to count items they see.
  9. Praise and reward them when they count correctly.

Shapes Names

Instructions

  1. Teach your kids basic shapes: Circle, Square, Triangle, Rectangle.
  2. Use visual aids like shape flashcards or toys.
  3. Repeat shape names during playtime and daily activities.
  4. Sing songs and rhymes about shapes.
  5. Use coloring books with different shapes and let them color them in.
  6. Consistently point out shapes in everyday objects.
  7. Encourage your child to identify shapes they see.
  8. Praise and reward them when they correctly identify shapes.
